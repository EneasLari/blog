Changing my diet to vegeterian: after 4 months

I decided that I want to change, and I started from what I eat

Posted by Eneas Lari on December 8, 2020

In this article I want to discuss about my decision to change my diet and what happened to my body. I will express my personal opinion on this so if you are easilly offended please get the f* out of here.
Before 4 months I decided to change something very important for my life. I decided to stop eating meat. Only meta not dairy products, for two simple reasons the first is ethical reason and the second is for improving my health. I respect any form of life and any creature deserves to live so I always felt a little hyprocrite for eating meat as long as i dont have any health issues that demand eating meat.This doesnt mean that I believe that everyone who eats meats doesnt love animals or whatever,but you got the point I think. Next I will refer the benefits and disantvantages I observed in my body.

Benefits

As I was eating a lot of meat my whole life the first month was difficult for me. I have been around 100kg since I was 18 years old. For many years I was eating a lot and I was working out so I Was balancing my body's look, but I always wanted a more lean body. After I removed meat from my diet I saw a drastic change in my body. I am now 8kgs less with the same muscle mass, (yes I didnt became a skinny little bitch!) but I keep the same workout routine.
The big amount of meat I was eating made me feel that my energy level was low and always that was surprising to me because it should be the opposite. For that reason I was afraid that if I stop eating meat it will make me feel even more exhausted. Surprisingly after one month my energy level are more higher than before even if I my hunger feeling is more tense. My body is more "light", my body is not feeling stuffed and like it cant breathe, like it was when I was eating big amount of meat.
Also because I have some stress-related skin problems I was supposed to reduce fat consumption from my food. So eating meat doesnt help. After four months the skin related problems are in a good condition even if me stress levels this period of time is very high. They are not cured because the root of the problem is my stress level,which is high.
I wont mention the other medical benefits of removing the meat from your diet because you can google them and also I am not a doctor my self.But I assure you the benefits are a lot!

Disantvantages

One of the bigest challenges of not eating meat is finding What to eat. Ok now what I will eat with my rice or with my pasta. Solo rice or pasta is too plain. I love cooking, but I didnt know many recipes without meat. So I started googling. I started experimenting with mushrooms(not that kind of mushroom you pot head), brokoli, beans ,spinach and a lot of eggs. As I mentioned eggs I consume around 30 eggs(sorry chickens arround the world) a week, for me is too much but i dont think that for a bodybuilder is too much. Quickly the cooking became one of my favourite things of the day.
Another thing that I dont like as a non meat eater is the feeling of hunger. Is a little difficult to maintain your hunger level low without meat, but surpsisingly that is not a thing that makes you impractical of doing other things. The feeling of hunger is something that you can control and make your brain work pretty well and forget that you feel a little hungry. I mention that because no one has the time of being around veggies all the time when he/she feels hungry,unless you are a farmer or golf player(who loves grass...). So feeling hungry sometimes makes you unable to think something else than your hunger.
And the most important thing that I think that is the result of eating veggies is that my stomach is more prone to my anxiety and spicy things than before.I am not sure about this as I should see a doctor about this.

My experience from not eating meat is mainly positive with its downsides but every drastic change like this needs some adjustment time.I dont regret my decision because it makes me feel proud of my self for ethical reasons and because it has helped me to achieve my body goals.

Keep in mind that I am not a doctor and I would not tell someone to stop eating meat without being sure that there is no medical reason to eat meat(which is something I want to write a whole article about).
